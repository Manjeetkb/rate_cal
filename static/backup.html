Flavor in food products determines the sensory qualities of a food whilst the appearance and color of a food provide the first indicator of quality. Flavor and texture in food products are critical in confirming or undermining initial impression of our perception to the food. Wine has been very popular and an important drink since ages having several nutrition values and plays an integral part in our culture. Wine is a complex mixture housing many aroma and flavor compounds giving it a unique texture and bouquet. Out of many chemical compounds identified in grapes and wines, only a few compounds actually contribute to sensory perception of wine flavor. These volatile organic compounds (VOCs), if present below sensory threshold limits, may contribute positively to wine quality; however, excessive amounts can detract from quality, and are considered as a fault in wine.
