Compound Identification using PTR-MS

The concentration of compound [M] can be obtained If the rate coefficient k and reaction time t of the reaction is already known. This is achieved by employing the reaction kinetics to the ion-molecule reactions given below. $$\rm AH^{+}+\rm M \longrightarrow \rm MH^{+}+\rm A\,, \qquad \text{Proton transfer}$$ $$\rm A^{\pm}+\rm M \longrightarrow \rm M^{\pm}+ \rm A\,, \qquad \text{Electron tranfer}$$ $2^{nd}$ order rate equation: $$[{\rm M}] = \frac{1}{\rm k\tau} \frac{[{\rm RH}^{+}]}{[{\rm H}_3{\rm O}^{+}]_o}\,.$$ The rate coefficient of the reaction k can be calculated using dipole collision theory in which dipole moment and polarizability of the neutral molecule are input ingredients.
